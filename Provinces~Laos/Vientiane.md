## #has_/text_of_/abstract 

> **Vientiane** (Lao: ວຽງຈັນ, romanized: Viang Chan, lit. 'sandalwood town', RTGS: Wiang Chan, 
> pronounced [wíaŋ tɕàn]) is the capital and largest city of Laos. 
> 
> Situated on the banks of the Mekong River at the Thai border, 
> it comprises the five urban districts of Vientiane Prefecture 
> and had a population of 840,000 as of the 2023 Census. 
> 
> Established as the capital of the Kingdom of Lan Xang in 1563, 
> Vientiane served as the administrative center during French rule 
> and retains colonial-era architecture alongside Buddhist landmarks such as Pha That Luang, 
> a national symbol of Buddhism, and Haw Phra Kaew, 
> which once housed the Emerald Buddha until its 18th-century relocation to Thailand.
>
> The city functions as Laos' political, economic and transportation hub, 
> emphasizing regional connectivity through infrastructure projects like the Laos–China Railway (LCR). 
> 
> This railway, a component of China's Belt and Road Initiative, terminates in Vientiane from [[../../../Asia~East/China/provinces~China/Yunnan/counties~Yunnan/Kunming,County/cities~Kunming/Kunming,City|Kunming,City]], China, 
> and is slated to connect to Thailand’s rail network via the Mekong Railway Bridge.
>
